Gameplay

The Last of Us is an action-adventure game played from a third-person perspective. The player traverses post-apocalyptic environments such as towns, buildings, forests, and sewers to advance the story. The player can use firearms, improvised weapons, hand-to-hand combat, and stealth to defend against hostile humans and cannibalistic creatures infected by a mutated strain of the Cordyceps fungus. For most of the game, the player takes control of Joel, a man tasked with escorting a young girl, Ellie, across the United States.The player also controls Ellie throughout the game's winter segment and briefly controls Joel's daughter, Sarah, in the opening sequence.

Plot

In a post-apocalyptic world, a smuggler named Joel is tasked with escorting a teenage girl named Ellie across a dangerous and infected United States, as she is immune to the fungal infection that has ravaged the world. They face various challenges and dangers along the way, including infected creatures and hostile human factions, but eventually form a strong bond. Joel discovers that creating a vaccine from Ellie's immunity would require her to die, and he chooses to rescue her instead, killing the Fireflies who were going to perform the operation. The game ends with Joel lying to Ellie about the events that transpired, and the two continue their journey together.

History of creation

The Last of Us was developed by Naughty Dog, a video game studio known for creating popular game series such as Crash Bandicoot, Jak and Daxter, and Uncharted. Development of the game began in 2009, with a small team led by creative director Neil Druckmann and game director Bruce Straley.


The idea for the game came from a conversation between Druckmann and his friend, discussing the idea of a post-apocalyptic world where the player would have to care for a child. The team then began developing the story and characters, drawing inspiration from various sources such as The Road and Children of Men.


The game was officially announced in 2011, and the development team worked for several more years to perfect the game's mechanics, graphics, and storyline. The game was released in June 2013 to critical acclaim and commercial success, winning numerous awards and becoming one of the best-selling video games of all time.


In 2014, the game was remastered and re-released for the PlayStation 4, and a sequel, The Last of Us Part II, was released in June 2020.

Music

The music of The Last of Us was composed by Gustavo Santaolalla, an Argentine musician known for his work in film and television. Santaolalla was chosen by the game's developers to create a score that would capture the emotional depth and tension of the game's storyline.


The score features acoustic guitar, percussion, and atmospheric sounds, often incorporating Latin American influences. Santaolalla's use of minimalistic themes and organic instrumentation create a haunting and intimate atmosphere that reflects the game's post-apocalyptic setting and emotional narrative.


The game's main theme, "The Last of Us", has become iconic and is often cited as one of the best video game themes of all time. The soundtrack was released in June 2013 and was met with critical acclaim, winning numerous awards, including the BAFTA Award for Best Music in 2014.


The music of The Last of Us Part II was also composed by Gustavo Santaolalla, along with additional music by Mac Quayle. The score builds on the themes established in the first game, incorporating new instrumentation and expanded soundscapes to reflect the game's broader scope and emotional complexity.

Feedbacks

The Last of Us received widespread critical acclaim upon its release in 2013. It was praised for its storytelling, characters, gameplay mechanics, and overall presentation. Some notable critic reviews include:


  • IGN's Colin Moriarty gave the game a perfect score of 10 out of 10, stating that "The Last of Us is a masterpiece, PlayStation 3's swan song, and a game that will be talked about for years to come."

  • Polygon's Arthur Gies gave the game a score of 9.5 out of 10, praising the game's character development, emotional depth, and gameplay mechanics, stating that "The Last of Us is an incredible game, and one of the finest experiences you can have on PlayStation 3."

  • Eurogamer's Oli Welsh gave the game a score of 10 out of 10, calling it "a landmark game, a sobering story with characters worth caring about, world-building detail worthy of a novel, and gameplay that never ceases to surprise and challenge the player."

Overall, The Last of Us is widely regarded as one of the best video games of all time and has received numerous awards and accolades for its storytelling, characters, and gameplay mechanics.
